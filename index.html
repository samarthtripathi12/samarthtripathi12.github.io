PROJECT 01 — JUL 2023 – OCT 2023

Black Hole Light Bending

Computational General Relativity

Starting point: the Schwarzschild metric written on paper, and the question of whether photons actually curve the way Einstein predicted. Derived the geodesic equations from first principles — no textbook shortcuts — then numerically integrated photon trajectories using 4th-order Runge-Kutta. The photon sphere at r = 1.5Rₛ emerged from the math, not a lookup. Final verification: sub-10⁻³ relative error against the analytic deflection angle. Three months from equation derivation to confirmed simulation.

PROJECT 02 — AUG 2023 – NOV 2023

Gravitational Wave Denoising

LIGO Real Data · Signal Processing

The raw GW150914 H1 strain file is noise. The gravitational wave — a spacetime distortion smaller than a proton — is buried inside it. Built a full analysis pipeline from scratch: derived Fourier transform equations independently, applied FFT spectral decomposition, then designed and tuned a 4th-order Butterworth bandpass filter at 50–600 Hz. The chirp emerges. SNR rises from 3.85 raw to 4.02 filtered. The Nobel Prize audio — the actual sound of two black holes merging 1.3 billion light years away — reconstructed entirely in Python.

PROJECT 03 — OCT 2023 – JAN 2024

Neural Network From Scratch

Deep Learning · Algorithmic Foundations

No TensorFlow. No PyTorch. No ML frameworks of any kind — only NumPy and the chain rule. Built every component by hand: forward propagation, backpropagation, gradient descent weight updates, all equations independently derived. The network learns to solve XOR — a problem that famously broke early perceptrons — achieving 99.5% accuracy (199/200). Four random seeds trained simultaneously reveal the Markov-chain geometry of gradient descent: different starting points, same attractor basin, same truth.

PROJECT 04 — NOV 2023 – FEB 2024

Chaotic Systems & Sensitivity

The double pendulum and Lorenz attractor share one property: they are deterministic systems that are practically unpredictable. Simulated both from scratch and computed Lyapunov exponents numerically to quantify exactly how fast nearby trajectories diverge. The Lorenz system produces a strange attractor with fractal basin boundaries — structure hiding inside apparent randomness. The key insight after three months of work: determinism and unpredictability are not opposites. They coexist in the same equations. Chaos is geometry you haven’t found yet.

PROJECT 05 — JAN 2024 – APR 2024

Monte Carlo Simulation of Physical Laws

The core question: can randomness verify truth? Built a stochastic simulation framework that recovers foundational physical laws — Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distributions, ideal gas pressure, thermodynamic equilibria — purely through random sampling and convergence analysis, with no analytic derivation. At high enough trial counts, the noise collapses into certainty. The result is not just a numerical method — it is a proof that the laws of physics are attractors that randomness converges toward, given enough iterations.

PROJECT 06 — MAR 2024 – JUL 2024

Quantitative Finance via Monte Carlo

The same stochastic framework from physics, applied to financial derivatives. Simulated thousands of asset price paths using geometric Brownian motion, then priced European call and put options by averaging discounted payoffs across paths. Cross-validated every result against the Black-Scholes closed-form solution. The deeper result was the one that mattered: thermodynamic diffusion and financial uncertainty are formally identical. The same Wiener process that describes a particle in a fluid governs the price of an option. Physics and finance share an equation.

PROJECT 07 — AUG 2024 – OCT 2024

Data Science — Global Temperature Anomaly

A century of NASA GISS global surface temperature anomaly data. The goal: extract the long-term warming signal from year-to-year noise without cherry-picking the window. Applied linear and polynomial regression, moving-average smoothing, and residual analysis. Result: a warming trend of ~0.19°C per decade since 1975 — recovered independently from raw data, consistent with published IPCC figures. The exercise was as much about methodology as outcome: letting data speak without selecting the frame that confirms the answer you want.

PROJECT 08 — NOV 2024 – FEB 2025

Energy-Inspired Portfolio Optimization

Portfolio optimization reframed as a physical energy minimisation problem. Assets modelled as particles in a potential energy landscape defined by expected return and covariance; the optimizer — built on stochastic differential equations analogous to simulated annealing — seeks the lowest-energy configuration. The approach recovers the Markowitz efficient frontier from first principles. The geometric insight: mean-variance optimization is not a financial formula. It is a statement about a curved surface in return-risk space, and the optimal portfolio is simply its lowest point.
